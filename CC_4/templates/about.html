Understanding Heart Disease

Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease, is a group of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It's the leading cause of death worldwide, but many cases can be prevented through lifestyle changes and early detection.

Common Types of Heart Disease:
  • Coronary Artery Disease: Narrowing of blood vessels
  • Heart Attack: Blockage of blood flow to heart
  • Heart Failure: Heart can't pump blood effectively
  • Arrhythmia: Irregular heart rhythm
  • Valve Disease: Problems with heart valves
  • Cardiomyopathy: Heart muscle disease
  • Pericardial Disease: Inflammation of heart lining
  • Congenital Heart Disease: Birth defects

Risk Factors for Heart Disease

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors
  • Age: Risk increases with age
  • Gender: Men have higher risk
  • Family History: Genetic predisposition
  • Ethnicity: Some groups have higher risk
Modifiable Risk Factors
  • High Blood Pressure: Hypertension
  • High Cholesterol: Elevated LDL levels
  • Diabetes: Poor blood sugar control
  • Obesity: Excess body weight
  • Smoking: Tobacco use
  • Physical Inactivity: Sedentary lifestyle
  • Poor Diet: Unhealthy eating habits
  • Stress: Chronic stress

Assessment Parameters Explained

Basic Health Metrics
  • Age: Risk increases with age, especially after 45 for men and 55 for women
  • Sex: Men generally have higher risk than premenopausal women
  • Blood Pressure: High BP damages arteries and increases heart workload
  • Cholesterol: High LDL cholesterol leads to plaque buildup
  • Blood Sugar: Diabetes significantly increases cardiovascular risk
Cardiac-Specific Measures
  • Chest Pain Type: Different patterns indicate various heart conditions
  • Exercise Angina: Chest pain during physical activity
  • Resting ECG: Electrical activity of the heart
  • Max Heart Rate: Peak heart rate during exercise
  • ST Depression: ECG changes indicating heart stress
  • ST Slope: Pattern of ST segment changes

How It Works

Our Random Forest model analyzes multiple decision trees to predict heart disease risk. Each tree considers different combinations of your health parameters, and the final prediction is based on the majority vote from all trees, providing a robust and accurate assessment.
